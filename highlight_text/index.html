Buscar

Anna Metselitsa is the 30-year-old female founder of Haute Rogue, which is an online boutique with a large following and designs sold in exclusive and renowned stores. This would be quite an achievement for any business owner, but it is a great success for an immigrant entrepreneur who came to the United States with just $300 in her pocket. She left Belarus for America in the year 2007, ultimately finding her way to New York City. For about 7 years, she waitressed and had many jobs in order to survive and save for her dream of becoming a fashion designer. Her dream came true in May 2014, when she launched her online shop.

Metselitsa did not have an easy childhood as her family lived off government assistance and purchased only the bare necessities. What kept her going was her passion for clothing, cultivated early on through fashion magazines, which she bought whenever she could and which inspired her to find low-cost ways to look good, even if her creations did not last long. Likewise, she also found low-cost ways to look good even if her creations did not last long.

After moving to New York, she taught herself website design and coding using online resources and initially sold clothing made by other designers, while she worked on designs for dresses and shirts. Saying she struggled at the beginning would be an understatement. Sleeping on a warehouse floor by night and eating cheap fast food by day, Nevertheless, Haute Rogue grew. Haute Rogue now sells products through more than 800 stores across North America, and she now plans to expand into other continents.

For two years, she experienced some complications in keeping her business afloat, specifically with reinvesting her income she made from the business. She also began to sell her own colorful designs to numerous small New York boutiques. By 2016, everything was looking up, with the business receiving its first big order from the fashion giant Forever 21.

Partnering with a friend from the wholesale sector, she was able to keep Haute Rogue on budget and has, since then, seen it grow with each passing year. Today, its clothing is sold in stores in the U.S., Canada and Mexico, while the company’s online presence now includes more than 77,000 followers on Instagram and 15,000 on Facebook. Whatever the future holds, Metselitsa still has big dreams for new opportunities.
